Award-Winning Curriculum Design

Evaluated against the standards of the top 0.01% of curriculum developers worldwide, the RootWork Framework meets or exceeds expectations in evidence-based practices, cognitive rigor, and immediate adoptability.

Trauma-Informed Care

Grounded in SAMHSA's six key principles and neuroscience research on Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs), with explicit integration of the 5 Rs: Rooting, Regulating, Reflecting, Restoring, and Reconnecting.

STEAM Integration

Comprehensive coverage of NGSS, Common Core Math/ELA, and authentic interdisciplinary learning through Living Learning Labs and therapeutic horticulture.

Universal Design for Learning

Multiple pathways to engagement, representation, and expression. Explicit support for 504, SPED, Gifted/Talented, and ELL learners with trauma-informed modifications.

Assessment & Continuous Improvement

Curriculum-Based Measurement (CBM) protocols, formative assessment checkpoints, and data-driven progress monitoring aligned with AERA/APA/NCME standards.

Cultural Sustainability

Goes beyond cultural responsiveness to culturally sustaining pedagogy, honoring diverse heritage, indigenous knowledge, and community wisdom.

Legal Compliance & Audit-Ready

Developed by an attorney with Ed.D. and EBD endorsement. Full compliance with IDEA, Section 504, FERPA, and HIPAA requirements with comprehensive documentation.

About the RootWork Framework

The RootWork Framework K-12 Curriculum represents a paradigm shift in education, seamlessly integrating therapeutic horticulture, trauma-informed pedagogy, and rigorous academic standards. Developed by Dr. Shawn Hearn—an educator, attorney, and military veteran with specialized training in special education and emotional/behavioral disorders—this curriculum addresses the urgent need for healing-centered, culturally sustaining educational experiences.
