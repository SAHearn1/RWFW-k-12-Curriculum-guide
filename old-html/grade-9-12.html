Grades 9-12: Impact & Innovation

Professional-level research, sustainability leadership, career pathway development, and community impact through advanced garden-based science.

Generate 9-12 Lesson Explore Curriculum

Developmental Overview

High school students engage in professional-level research, develop sophisticated understanding of sustainability science, create community impact through capstone projects, and build career pathways in environmental and agricultural fields.

Senior Capstone Projects

Students design and implement year-long research projects addressing authentic community needs, demonstrating mastery across multiple disciplines.

Career Pathway Exploration

Students explore careers in agriculture, environmental science, sustainability, public health, and related fields through mentorships, internships, and job shadowing.

Advanced Data Science

Students collect and analyze complex datasets, use statistical software, create data visualizations, and draw evidence-based conclusions for real-world applications.

Sustainability Leadership

Students develop solutions to sustainability challenges, engage in policy advocacy, and lead initiatives that transform their schools and communities.

Standards Alignment

Comprehensive alignment with NGSS, Common Core Math and ELA, and CASEL Social-Emotional Learning competencies.

The 5 Rs: Trauma-Informed Framework

The RootWork Framework integrates SAMHSA's trauma-informed principles through five interconnected practices.

Rooting

Grounding in Evidence

Students ground their investigations in systematic observation and data collection, building evidence-based thinking skills.

Regulating

Self-Management

Garden activities provide opportunities for stress management, mindfulness, and healthy coping strategies.

Reflecting

Metacognition

Students analyze their learning processes, evaluate evidence critically, and refine their scientific thinking.

Restoring

Wellness Practices

The garden offers restorative spaces for processing, recovery, and sustainable learning practices.

Reconnecting

Collaborative Learning

Shared projects build authentic community connections and develop leadership skills.

Core Learning Themes

Senior Research Thesis

Duration: Full year | Focus: Independent research project

Students design and conduct original research on self-selected topics, following professional scientific protocols. They present findings at regional science fairs, submit to student journals, and defend their work before panels.

Key Activities: Literature review, IRB protocols, data collection, statistical analysis, research paper writing, thesis defense, conference presentations

Climate Change & Food Systems

Duration: 10-12 weeks | Focus: Sustainability science

Students investigate relationships between climate change and agricultural systems, exploring adaptation strategies, resilience building, and sustainable food production methods at multiple scales.

Key Activities: Climate modeling, carbon footprint analysis, sustainable agriculture research, adaptation strategy design, policy analysis

Public Health & Food Access

Duration: 10-12 weeks | Focus: Community health equity

Students examine intersections between food systems, public health, and health equity. They conduct community health assessments and design interventions addressing food-related health disparities.

Key Activities: Epidemiological research, community health surveys, intervention design, health outcome analysis, policy recommendations

Innovation & Entrepreneurship

Duration: 10-12 weeks | Focus: Social enterprise development

Students develop sustainable social enterprises or innovative solutions to agricultural and environmental challenges, creating business plans, prototypes, and implementation strategies.

Key Activities: Market research, business plan development, prototype creation, pitch competitions, implementation planning

Assessment Strategies

Comprehensive assessment approaches that honor diverse learning pathways and demonstrate growth over time.

Performance-Based Assessment

Students demonstrate mastery through authentic tasks, projects, and presentations that apply learning to real-world contexts.

Portfolio Development

Students curate evidence of learning across multiple domains, reflecting on growth and setting goals.

Progress Monitoring

Regular formative assessments track skill development, inform instruction, and support intervention.

Peer & Self-Assessment

Students develop metacognitive skills through structured reflection and constructive peer feedback.
