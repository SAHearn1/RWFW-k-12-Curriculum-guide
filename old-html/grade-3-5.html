Grades 3-5: Investigation & Connection

Deepening scientific inquiry skills, exploring ecosystem relationships, and developing data-driven investigation through hands-on garden science.

Generate 3-5 Lesson Explore Curriculum

Developmental Overview

Grades 3-5 students transition from simple observation to systematic investigation, exploring ecosystem relationships, conducting controlled experiments, and analyzing data to answer increasingly complex scientific questions.

Scientific Inquiry Methods

Students design and conduct investigations, forming testable hypotheses, collecting quantitative data, and drawing evidence-based conclusions.

Ecosystem Interdependence

Students explore complex relationships between organisms and their environment, understanding food webs, decomposition cycles, and nutrient flows.

Data Collection & Analysis

Students measure variables, record observations systematically, create graphs and charts, and interpret quantitative patterns.

Cultural Heritage Gardens

Students explore agricultural traditions from diverse cultures, connecting scientific learning to cultural identity and community wisdom.

Standards Alignment

Comprehensive alignment with NGSS, Common Core Math and ELA, and CASEL Social-Emotional Learning competencies.

The 5 Rs: Trauma-Informed Framework

The RootWork Framework integrates SAMHSA's trauma-informed principles through five interconnected practices.

Rooting

Grounding in Evidence

Students ground their investigations in systematic observation and data collection, building evidence-based thinking skills.

Regulating

Self-Management

Garden activities provide opportunities for stress management, mindfulness, and healthy coping strategies.

Reflecting

Metacognition

Students analyze their learning processes, evaluate evidence critically, and refine their scientific thinking.

Restoring

Wellness Practices

The garden offers restorative spaces for processing, recovery, and sustainable learning practices.

Reconnecting

Collaborative Learning

Shared projects build authentic community connections and develop leadership skills.

Core Learning Themes

Ecosystem Connections: Food Webs in Action

Duration: 8-10 weeks | Focus: Interdependent relationships in ecosystems

Students investigate complex food webs in the garden ecosystem, exploring producer, consumer, and decomposer relationships. They track energy flow and material cycling through direct observation and experimentation.

Key Activities: Food web mapping, decomposition experiments, predator-prey simulations, energy pyramid construction, soil food web investigations

Plant Science: Structure & Function

Duration: 6-8 weeks | Focus: Plant anatomy and adaptations

Students conduct systematic investigations of plant structures, exploring how roots, stems, leaves, flowers, and seeds function to support survival and reproduction. Comparative anatomy reveals adaptations to different environments.

Key Activities: Plant dissection, transpiration experiments, photosynthesis investigations, seed dispersal studies, adaptation comparisons

Water Cycle & Conservation

Duration: 6-8 weeks | Focus: Water systems and stewardship

Students explore the water cycle through garden-based observations and experiments, investigating water conservation strategies and developing solutions to water-related challenges in their school and community.

Key Activities: Water cycle models, irrigation system design, rainwater harvesting, soil water retention experiments, watershed mapping

Cultural Heritage & Agricultural Traditions

Duration: 8-10 weeks | Focus: Cultural sustainability through agriculture

Students research and cultivate heritage crops from diverse cultural traditions, interviewing community members and learning traditional agricultural knowledge passed down through generations.

Key Activities: Heritage crop cultivation, elder interviews, traditional farming technique research, cultural celebration events, family recipe projects

Assessment Strategies

Comprehensive assessment approaches that honor diverse learning pathways and demonstrate growth over time.

Performance-Based Assessment

Students demonstrate mastery through authentic tasks, projects, and presentations that apply learning to real-world contexts.

Portfolio Development

Students curate evidence of learning across multiple domains, reflecting on growth and setting goals.

Progress Monitoring

Regular formative assessments track skill development, inform instruction, and support intervention.

Peer & Self-Assessment

Students develop metacognitive skills through structured reflection and constructive peer feedback.
