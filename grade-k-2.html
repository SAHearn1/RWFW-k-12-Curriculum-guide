Grades K-2: Wonder & Discovery

Building foundational skills through sensory exploration, emotional regulation, and joyful discovery in the garden.

Generate K-2 Lesson Explore Curriculum

Developmental Overview

The K-2 RootWork Framework centers on wonder and discovery, recognizing that young learners are naturally curious explorers. Through hands-on garden experiences, students develop foundational skills in observation, emotional regulation, and collaborative learning while building essential academic competencies.

Sensory Exploration

Students engage all five senses to observe and describe plants, soil, weather, and seasonal changes, building vocabulary and descriptive language skills.

Emotional Regulation

Through garden-based mindfulness activities and community circle practices, students develop foundational self-regulation and social-emotional skills.

Life Cycles & Patterns

Students observe and document plant growth, seasonal patterns, and basic life cycles, developing scientific thinking and mathematical pattern recognition.

Community Building

Garden work becomes a shared experience where students learn cooperation, responsibility, and respect for living things and each other.

Standards Alignment

K-2 curriculum is comprehensively aligned with Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS), Common Core Math and ELA, and CASEL Social-Emotional Learning competencies.

NGSS Science Standards

K-LS1-1

Use observations to describe patterns of what plants and animals (including humans) need to survive.

K-ESS2-1

Use and share observations of local weather conditions to describe patterns over time.

K-ESS3-1

Use a model to represent the relationship between the needs of different plants or animals and the places they live.

1-LS1-1

Use materials to design solutions to human problems by mimicking how plants and/or animals use their external parts to help them survive.

1-LS3-1

Make observations to construct an evidence-based account that young plants and animals are like, but not exactly like, their parents.

2-LS2-1

Plan and conduct an investigation to determine if plants need sunlight and water to grow.

Common Core ELA Standards

CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.W.K-2.2

Write informative/explanatory texts to examine and convey information about the garden and natural world.

CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K-2.1

Participate in collaborative conversations about grade-level topics with diverse partners during community circles.

CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.L.K-2.5

Demonstrate understanding of word relationships and nuances in word meanings related to nature and plants.

CASEL Social-Emotional Learning

Self-Awareness

Students identify and express emotions experienced in the garden, developing vocabulary for feelings and bodily sensations.

Self-Management

Students practice calming strategies during garden time, learning to regulate emotions through nature connection.

Social Awareness

Students demonstrate respect for living things and develop empathy through caring for plants and garden creatures.

The 5 Rs: Trauma-Informed Framework for K-2

The RootWork Framework integrates SAMHSA's trauma-informed principles through five interconnected practices, adapted developmentally for early elementary learners.

Rooting

Grounding in Observation

Students use all five senses to observe the garden, developing present-moment awareness and scientific observation skills.

K-2 Practice: "I notice..." sentence stems, sensory exploration stations, nature journaling with drawings and simple words.

Regulating

Emotional & Physical Regulation

Garden activities provide natural opportunities for movement, sensory input, and calming strategies that support self-regulation.

K-2 Practice: Deep breathing with flowers, mindful walking on garden paths, "zones of regulation" check-ins using garden visuals.

Reflecting

Thinking About Learning

Students share observations and wonderings, developing metacognitive awareness and scientific questioning skills.

K-2 Practice: "I wonder..." questions, turn-and-talk partnerships, simple exit tickets with pictures and words.

Restoring

Rest & Recovery

The garden provides natural spaces for rest, recovery, and sensory breaks, supporting students' physical and emotional needs.

K-2 Practice: "Garden rest spots," quiet observation time, gentle stretching activities modeled after plants growing.

Reconnecting

Building Community

Shared garden work creates authentic opportunities for cooperation, communication, and building positive relationships.

K-2 Practice: Partner planting activities, community circle sharing, celebrating growth together.

Core Learning Themes

What Do Plants Need?

Duration: 6-8 weeks | Science Focus: Basic needs of living things

Students investigate what plants need to survive through hands-on experiments with water, sunlight, and soil. They document observations using drawings, photos, and emergent writing.

Key Activities: Seed planting experiments, sunlight comparison studies, water measurement practice, plant growth journals

Seasons in Our Garden

Duration: Full year integration | Science Focus: Seasonal patterns and weather

Students observe and document seasonal changes in the garden, developing understanding of weather patterns, plant life cycles, and seasonal rhythms.

Key Activities: Seasonal observation journals, weather pattern tracking, seasonal planting calendar, life cycle observations

Garden Detectives: Observation Skills

Duration: 4-6 weeks | Science Focus: Scientific observation and inquiry

Students develop systematic observation skills using all five senses, learning to describe, compare, and classify plants, insects, and garden features.

Key Activities: Sensory exploration stations, observation journals, classification games, descriptive writing practice

From Seed to Plant

Duration: 8-10 weeks | Science Focus: Life cycles and plant growth

Students observe complete plant life cycles from seed germination through flowering and seed production, developing understanding of growth, change, and life patterns.

Key Activities: Seed dissection, germination observations, growth measurement, life cycle sequencing, plant anatomy exploration

Developmentally Appropriate Assessment

K-2 assessment emphasizes observation, documentation, and celebration of growth rather than traditional testing, honoring young learners' development.

Nature Journals

Students document observations through drawings, emergent writing, photos, and labeled diagrams, showing growth in scientific observation and literacy skills.

Community Circle Sharing

Regular opportunities to share observations, questions, and discoveries assess oral language development and scientific thinking.

Photo Documentation

Teacher-captured photos document student engagement, skill development, and growth over time, supporting portfolio assessment.

Observation Checklists

Teachers use developmentally appropriate checklists to track skill progression in areas like observation, inquiry, and cooperation.

Growth Over Time

Portfolio assessment tracks individual student growth across the year, celebrating progress in academic and social-emotional domains.

Family Engagement

Take-home activities and family garden events provide additional assessment data while building home-school connections.
