Grades 6-8: Systems & Leadership

Developing systems thinking, environmental justice awareness, and student-led community change through advanced garden-based science.

Generate 6-8 Lesson Explore Curriculum

Developmental Overview

Middle school students engage in complex systems thinking, exploring environmental justice issues, conducting advanced experimental designs, and leading community-based projects that create measurable impact.

Complex Systems Thinking

Students analyze interconnected systems including carbon cycling, water systems, food systems, and social-ecological relationships at multiple scales.

Environmental Justice

Students investigate food deserts, environmental racism, inequitable access to green space, and develop advocacy skills for systemic change.

Advanced Experimental Design

Students design multi-variable experiments, control for confounding factors, use statistical analysis, and present findings to authentic audiences.

Student-Led Initiatives

Students identify community needs, design sustainable solutions, secure resources, and implement projects that benefit their school and broader community.

Standards Alignment

Comprehensive alignment with NGSS, Common Core Math and ELA, and CASEL Social-Emotional Learning competencies.

The 5 Rs: Trauma-Informed Framework

The RootWork Framework integrates SAMHSA's trauma-informed principles through five interconnected practices.

Rooting

Grounding in Evidence

Students ground their investigations in systematic observation and data collection, building evidence-based thinking skills.

Regulating

Self-Management

Garden activities provide opportunities for stress management, mindfulness, and healthy coping strategies.

Reflecting

Metacognition

Students analyze their learning processes, evaluate evidence critically, and refine their scientific thinking.

Restoring

Wellness Practices

The garden offers restorative spaces for processing, recovery, and sustainable learning practices.

Reconnecting

Collaborative Learning

Shared projects build authentic community connections and develop leadership skills.

Core Learning Themes

Matter & Energy: Biogeochemical Cycles

Duration: 8-10 weeks | Focus: Complex systems and cycling

Students investigate carbon, nitrogen, and water cycles through multi-scale observations and experiments. They model matter transformation and energy transfer, connecting garden-scale phenomena to global systems.

Key Activities: Carbon footprint analysis, composting system design, nitrogen cycle experiments, greenhouse gas monitoring, watershed impact studies

Food Justice & Sustainable Systems

Duration: 10-12 weeks | Focus: Environmental justice and advocacy

Students conduct community food system audits, investigating food access inequities, food deserts, and environmental racism. They design and implement solutions that increase food security and environmental justice.

Key Activities: Food desert mapping, community needs assessment, policy research, advocacy campaigns, sustainable food system design

Scientific Investigation & Experimental Design

Duration: 8-10 weeks | Focus: Advanced research methods

Students design and conduct original research investigating garden-based questions. They control variables, collect quantitative data, perform statistical analysis, and present findings at student research conferences.

Key Activities: Hypothesis testing, multi-variable experiments, data visualization, statistical analysis, scientific poster presentations

Community-Based Participatory Research

Duration: 12-16 weeks | Focus: Student-led community projects

Students partner with community organizations to identify needs, design research protocols, collect data, analyze findings, and implement solutions that address real community challenges.

Key Activities: Community partner meetings, research design, data collection protocols, solution implementation, impact assessment

Assessment Strategies

Comprehensive assessment approaches that honor diverse learning pathways and demonstrate growth over time.

Performance-Based Assessment

Students demonstrate mastery through authentic tasks, projects, and presentations that apply learning to real-world contexts.

Portfolio Development

Students curate evidence of learning across multiple domains, reflecting on growth and setting goals.

Progress Monitoring

Regular formative assessments track skill development, inform instruction, and support intervention.

Peer & Self-Assessment

Students develop metacognitive skills through structured reflection and constructive peer feedback.
